What I Believe . .

I believe in unity. Call me old fashioned but I still believe that it takes a village to raise a child. Somewhere along the lines I feel we have lost the logic and moral behind this theory. I believe that through educational/trade programs (similar to the ones taught at Harry Hill in past times), “positive” police integration into neighborhoods, affordable (free) community themed events, serious efforts to fight the housing and homelessness epidemic, and various forums to address the public voice, concerns, and opinions we can bring Lansing back to the residents. The way it was. The way it should be.

We have spent years, if not decades, following the same type of plans that got us in this situation in the first place. The situation where we feel like the city no longer belongs to us or even cares for our needs. The definition of insanity is trying the same thing over and over again and expecting different results. I don’t know about you but I’m tired of feeling like I’m going insane. It’s time for something different Lansing. I am a fresh perspective. I am not on the outside looking in as, I am deeply ingrained in Lansing. I am 41 now and have been here since I was 5. I’ve watched Lansing evolve over time. After speaking with many residents in our communities, city officials, and officers of the law I have a clear mind set that says it’s time for growth in Lansing.
